  • Little Brother by Cory Doctorow

Thoughts on Ebooks Vs. Physical Books

This year I went back to reading eBooks, actually, quite a few of these books I read on my iPad. The main advantages are:

  • I like being able to read in bed with lights out. I simply enable dark mode and I can read without disturbing my partner in her sleep
  • I like that the books I read don't take any physical space at home
  • I simply take the iPad with me and I'm able to read any book, so weight is not a concern (beyond the weigth of the iPad, of course)
  • I like that I can highlight parts of the text and search within the book very easily
  • Ebooks can't get lost, stained, or otherwise abused when traveling
  • When moving to another city (which I've done a few times) I don't need to worry about the weight of books or what to do with them
  • Ebooks tend to be cheaper than physical books, so there's a bit of savings there

The reason I prefer physical books in some cases:

  • I actually like having books in my bookshelf. It looks very intellectual, although I think I need a second bookshelf, hence eBooks are good sometimes
  • When it comes to learning I think it's better to have a physical book. For me, it's simpler to go back and forth, look at the index, find the page, etc. It's easier for reference. If I could combine that with an electronic search like in an eBook that would be awesome, but still a few years away, I guess
  • I can support a local business by buying in local bookstores. I'll only order from Amazon when I can't find the book locally, even if Amazon is cheaper

Of course it's a matter of preference, however, a few years ago I sweared off eBooks because I didn't feel like the content "stuck" as much as with a physical book. There's research that suggests physical books are better for memorizing the content. I convinced myself eBooks were not the way to go. Actually today in 2020, after doing a quick search (in DuckDuckGo, of course) I cannot find any recent studies on the topic. In any case, from these books some were physical and some were electronic. I enjoyed reading both and in order to boost my memory and keep an idea log I started this page.
