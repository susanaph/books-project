My books of 2020

At the end of last year I decided to quit social media, I closed by Facebook account and decided to use that time to read books. One topic that got my attention was data privacy, and you will see throughout the year I've read many books on the topic.

Most of the books I read I would recommend, and if not I will say it in the summary.

  1. Permanent Record by Edward Snowden
  2. Social Network Analysis by Song Yang, Franziska B. Keller, and Lu Zheng
  3. The People Vs. Tech by Jamie Bartlett
  4. Data and Goliath by Bruce Schneier
  5. Little Brother by Cory Doctorow
  6. Homeland by Cory Doctorrow
  7. Republic, Lost by Lawrence Lessig
  8. The Age of Surveillance Capitalism by Shoshana Zuboff
  9. The Human Network by Matthew O. Jackson
  10. Dying for a Paycheck by Jeffrey Pfeffer
  11. Unlocking Happiness at work by Jennifer Moss
  12. Born a Crime by Trevor Noah
  13. Small Fry by Lisa Brennan-Jobs
  14. Countdown to zero day by Kim Zetter
  15. The Professional Product Owner by Don McGreal and Ralph Jocham
  16. The Art of Invisibility by Kevin Mitnick
